High IQ

High IQ is DTF Genetics’ cannabis knowledge challenge: a structured trivia game designed to test plant science, cultivation, genetics, history, terminology, post-harvest practices, and responsible industry knowledge.

Return to the DTF Game Hub

Game format

  • Four difficulty levels: Easy, Medium, Hard, and Expert.
  • Four answer choices: A, B, C, and D.
  • One best answer: distractors must remain believable and connected to the same subject.
  • Separate answer key: the correct response and explanation are kept away from the question face.
  • Physical and digital direction: the same verified question database supports printable cards and a future browser version.

Knowledge areas

Plant biology

Cells, tissues, roots, shoots, leaves, flowers, photosynthesis, respiration, water movement, hormones, reproduction, and plant responses.

Environment and lighting

Temperature, humidity, vapor pressure deficit, air movement, photoperiod, spectrum, PPFD, DLI, fixture placement, and stress.

Water, roots, and nutrition

Water quality, pH, EC/PPM, media, irrigation, dryback, oxygen, nutrient roles, interactions, deficiencies, toxicities, and root-zone biology.

Cultivation practice

Germination, seedlings, cloning, vegetative growth, training, flowering, integrated pest management, crop observation, harvest, drying, curing, and storage.

Genetics and breeding

Genotype, phenotype, filial generations, selection, inheritance, variation, sex expression, pollination, seed development, and breeding records.

History, terminology, and culture

Documented cultivation history, commonly used terms, plant classification debates, landmark developments, and responsible adult-use culture.

Question quality standard

A question is not considered finished merely because it sounds plausible. Every production question must pass content, answer, and wording review.

  1. Confirm the question asks one clear thing.
  2. Verify the best answer with reliable sources or established plant science.
  3. Remove answers that are accidentally also correct.
  4. Use believable distractors rather than jokes or nonsense.
  5. State context when the answer changes by crop stage, environment, method, or measurement system.
  6. Write an explanation that teaches why the answer is correct.
  7. Review claims that could become outdated before release.
