Teaching Healthy Cultivation

Understand the plant. Build the environment. Make better decisions.

Teaching Healthy Cultivation is DTF Genetics' structured education system for plant biology, indoor and outdoor cultivation, crop observation, prevention, harvest, and breeding. The goal is practical understanding—not disconnected tips or unverified shortcuts.

Start with the foundations

  1. Plant biology: cells, roots, shoots, leaves, flowers, hormones, water movement, photosynthesis, respiration, and reproduction.
  2. Environment: temperature, humidity, vapor pressure deficit, air movement, carbon dioxide, sanitation, and grow-space design.
  3. Light: intensity, photoperiod, PPFD, DLI, spectrum, distance, uniformity, and plant response.
  4. Root zone: media, water quality, pH, EC/PPM, oxygen, dryback, irrigation, nutrient availability, and biology.
  5. Crop management: germination, seedlings, cloning, vegetative growth, training, flowering, observation, and recordkeeping.
  6. Protection and quality: integrated pest management, disease prevention, harvest timing, drying, curing, and storage.

Education library

Plant biology and genetics

Seed anatomy, germination, genotype and phenotype, sex expression, filial generations, selection, inheritance, variation, and breeding documentation.

Environment and VPD

Temperature, relative humidity, leaf temperature, vapor pressure deficit, air exchange, circulation, climate stability, and stage-appropriate targets.

Lighting

PPFD, DLI, photoperiod, spectrum, fixture placement, distribution, stress signals, and measuring light instead of guessing.

Water, pH, and EC

Source-water quality, alkalinity, dissolved minerals, irrigation volume, runoff, dryback, pH management, conductivity, and root-zone interpretation.

Nutrition and media

Primary, secondary, and micronutrients; nutrient interactions; soil, soilless, and hydroponic systems; cation exchange capacity; amendments; microbes; and organic matter.

Training and canopy management

Low-stress training, topping, pruning, high-stress training, SCROG, mainlining, support, plant spacing, and balancing light capture with airflow.

Integrated pest management

Exclusion, sanitation, scouting, identification, action thresholds, biological controls, environmental correction, resistance management, and pesticide-label compliance.

Harvest and post-harvest

Trichome observation, harvest readiness, handling, drying conditions, water activity concepts, curing, storage, and quality preservation.

Outdoor cultivation

Site selection, climate, hardening-off, transplanting, irrigation, wind, rain, wildlife, pollen drift, security, microclimates, and seasonal planning.

How the material is built

  • Separate plant science from personal preference.
  • State ranges and context instead of pretending one number fits every crop.
  • Distinguish symptoms from confirmed causes.
  • Explain what to observe, why it matters, and what action is justified.
  • Revise material when better evidence or clearer field documentation becomes available.
